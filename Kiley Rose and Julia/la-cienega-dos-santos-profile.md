# LA CIENEGA DOS SANTOS

## Character Profile

---

## BASIC INFORMATION

- **Full Name:** La Cienega dos Santos
- **Age at Relationship:** 21 (Kiley Rose is 19)
- **Nationality:** Brazilian
- **Relationship to Key Family:** Kiley Rose's second romantic partner

---

## RELATIONSHIP WITH KILEY ROSE

Kiley Rose meets La Cienega dos Santos at **Cassiopeia Stars** — a strip club where La Cienega works. Kiley Rose is nineteen, a young mother of toddler twin girls, carrying the weight of Lazaro's departure and the long aftermath of a pregnancy that nearly broke her. She is not looking for love. She finds it anyway.

La Cienega is twenty-one. She is confident, magnetic, and older than Kiley Rose in the ways that matter at that age — she knows who she is, she knows what she wants, and she is not afraid to pursue it. Their relationship begins in the dim light of Cassiopeia Stars and quickly becomes something more. Passionate. Consuming. The kind of romance that feels like a second chance at being young.

For two years, Kiley Rose and La Cienega build a life together. La Cienega is present in the twins' early childhood. She is a fixture in Kiley Rose's world. The relationship is intense and loving — a counterweight to the loneliness of single motherhood and the memory of a boy on a Rio beach who could not stay.

---

## THE BETRAYAL

When Kiley Rose is twenty-one, she walks in and finds La Cienega in bed with another woman.

It is not a misunderstanding. It is not a conversation that can be had. It is a body in a bed that was supposed to be shared only with her. The trust, built over two years, collapses in a single moment.

Kiley Rose does not stay to fight. She does not scream. She walks away — carrying the same quiet resolve that carried her through the pregnancy, through Lazaro's departure, through every hard thing she has ever faced. She has been unhurt before. She has been saved by love before. She has also been left by it. This time, she leaves first.

---

## AFTER

La Cienega does not appear to fight for Kiley Rose, or if she does, it is not enough. The relationship ends with the image of that bed burned into Kiley Rose's memory. It becomes the second scar she carries — not on her skin, like Jessica's burns, but in the part of her that learned that passion is not the same as loyalty.

---

## ARC AND CORE THEME

La Cienega dos Santos is the woman who taught Kiley Rose that love can be radiant and consuming — and that it can also break without warning. She is not the villain of Kiley Rose's story, but she is its hinge: the relationship that ended not because of circumstance or faith or a pregnancy, but because of a choice she made that Kiley Rose could not forgive.

She is the one who was caught. She is the one who left Kiley Rose with a new kind of scar. And she is the one who, in her absence, made room for Julia.
